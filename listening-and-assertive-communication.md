# Listening and Assertive Communication

### 1. Active Listening
**Que 1** What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?
- Avoid getting distracted during conversations.
- Focus on speaker.
- Don't interrupt when someone is speaking, let them finish and then talk.
- Give door openers.
- Focus on Body language.
- Take notes, if its a meeting or discussion.

### 2. Reflective Listening
**Que 2** According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?
- Focus on what the speaker is trying to convey.
- Give them time to express themselves. 
- Reflect what you understand without adding personal opinions.

### 3. Reflection
**Que 3** What are the obstacles in your listening process?
- Constantly checking of smartphones.
- Lack of interest in topic.
- Too much information at once.
- Having your own opinion without listening.

**Que 4** What can you do to improve your listening?
- Keep your opinion aside and focus on what's being said.
- Stay focused.
- Put yourself in the speaker’s perspective.

### 4. Types of Communication
**Que 5** When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?
- When talking to someone in a senior position.
- Fear of disapproval or rejection.
- Fear of hurting someone else's feelings.

**Que 6** When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?
- To get immediate results.
- Because of stress or frustration
- When feeling threatened.

**Que 7** When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?
- When not able to express themselves fully.
- Unspoken frustration.
- Fear of open or direct arguments.

**Que 8** How can you make your communication assertive? 
- Start with safe people and easy situations.
- Express your needs without apologising.
- Don’t wait, express yourself about things that are problematic.
- Stop putting other's feelings first all the time.
